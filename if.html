Interesting Facts about Guide Dogs

Guide dogs are true stand outs. Let's dig deep into the facts about this responsible and intelligent feature. Open each card below to explore in detail. These information will help you know them better.

Ancestors

A first-century mural dating from the Roman ruins of Herculaneum and a 13th century Chinese scroll are believed to be among the earliest depictions of dogs leading people who are blind.

Well Trained

Guide dogs are trained to lead around obstacles, including hazards like low branches that may be above the height of the dog but not of its owner. The dogs learn to be responsible for a space two times as wide and up to three times as tall as themselves to keep their owners safe. Dogs also learn to ignore distractions that interfere with their duties.

Things beyond ability

Guide dogs are truly smart, but there are some things they simply can't do. They can't read traffic signals, so the handler must determine when to proceed at a crosswalk. Some intersections have auditory signals, which help. And even though dogs have good homing instincts, they can't map out a route to an new location. The abilities of the dog and handler work in tandem to successfully navigate the world.
